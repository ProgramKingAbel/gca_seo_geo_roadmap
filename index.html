Key terminology

Understanding the Landscape

Three overlapping disciplines. Each has distinct mechanics, goals, and measurement approaches. Understanding the difference is the foundation of this strategy.

SEO Search Engine Optimisation
The practice of improving a website's visibility in traditional search engine results pages (SERPs). Built on signals like keywords, backlinks, technical health, site speed, and E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness). Goal: rank higher in a list of links. Still foundational — AI systems rely on indexed web content as a key input. Cannot be skipped.
AIO AI Overview Optimisation
Optimising to appear in Google's AI Overviews — the AI-generated summaries that appear above traditional results. Sits between SEO and GEO: it still relies heavily on traditional Google signals but rewards answer-first, clearly structured content. Appearing in AIO means being in Google's summary before anyone sees the blue links.
GEO Generative Engine Optimisation
Optimising to be cited or synthesised by AI platforms like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini. Not the same as SEO. Standard SEO metrics explain only 4–7% of citation variance in ChatGPT. GEO rewards: topical depth, clear explanatory prose, structured authority, consistent brand-topic association, and original data. Goal: be included in the answer, not just ranked.
E-E-A-T Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness
Google's quality framework for evaluating content credibility. Still relevant to GEO — AI systems prefer sources that demonstrate genuine subject-matter expertise and are consistently cited by others. For GCA, this means showcasing the verification process, industry partnerships (RCSA, ILO, GIZ), and real hiring outcomes.
Schema Structured Data / JSON-LD
Machine-readable code added to web pages that explicitly tells search engines and AI systems what content means. Types relevant to GCA: Organization, FAQ, HowTo, Article, DefinedTerm. Injected as JSON-LD in the page head or via Squarespace code blocks. Schema is how you make your content AI-parseable beyond plain text.
EOR Employer of Record
A third-party organisation that legally employs staff on behalf of a company in another country, handling contracts, payroll, tax, and compliance. GCA's model includes an EOR layer — a critical concept for international hiring content and a high-traffic query category GCA should own.
Prompt mapping AI Query Research
The GEO equivalent of keyword research. Instead of finding search volumes for terms, you identify the natural language questions buyers are typing into AI tools. Content is then engineered to directly answer these prompts.
Citation variance Why AI cites what it cites
Research shows only 4–7% of AI citation patterns are explained by traditional SEO metrics. The rest is driven by contextual relevance, semantic depth, consistency across sources, and authority signals that go beyond backlinks. This is why a dedicated GEO strategy is required alongside SEO.
1
SEO Foundations
Months 1–2 · Must complete before other phases can compound
Priority: Critical

Everything else depends on this. Technical SEO is the infrastructure that AI systems rely on when deciding which brands to reference. Without it, GEO content has weaker foundations. This phase requires no expensive tools — primarily Google's free suite and one crawl tool.

Core principle: AI systems often start from indexed web content. A site with broken links, slow load times, and missing meta data sends weak trust signals to both Google and AI engines.
Actionable steps
  • Submit sitemap to Google Search Console — verify ownership and submit sitemap.xml. Free. Takes 30 minutes. Baseline requirement.
  • Rewrite all page meta titles and descriptions — every page needs a descriptive, keyword-intentional title. E.g. "Hire Vetted African Talent Globally | Global Careers Africa" not "Home".
  • Crawl the site with Screaming Frog — identify 404s, redirect chains, missing alt tags, duplicate titles. Fix before building new content.
  • Check Core Web Vitals — use Google PageSpeed Insights. Fast FCP/TTI improves AI citation probability.
  • Expand thin pages to 400+ words of prose — homepage, Smartsourcing, Build a Global Team, Why Africa, Verification Promise. AI cannot synthesise image-heavy pages.
  • Add Organization schema to homepage — inject JSON-LD defining GCA's name, URL, founding, services, and partner organisations via Squarespace code injection.
  • Build a keyword map — 30–50 priority queries using Google Search Console, AlsoAsked.com, and AnswerThePublic. Map each to an existing or planned page.
  • Write partner credibility in prose — RCSA, ILO, GIZ, Rendeavour are shown as logos only. AI cannot read images. Write a 150-word explanation of each partnership.

2
AIO Readiness — Google AI Overviews
Months 2–5 · Runs alongside Phase 1 content work
Priority: High

Google's AI Overviews appear above organic results for many queries and still lean on traditional SEO signals — but require answer-first content structure. Winning here is the most accessible AI visibility target because GCA's core SEO work feeds directly into it.

Key insight: ChatGPT rewards depth and topical consistency. Perplexity pulls from fresh, niche, and forum-style content. Google AI Overviews still lean on traditional SEO signals. AIO is the bridge — optimising for it improves both traditional and AI performance simultaneously.
Prompt mapping — build your AI keyword list

Before writing a single word, map the 30–50 questions your buyers are asking AI tools. Examples for GCA:

  • "What are the best platforms to hire African talent?"
  • "How do I hire compliantly in Kenya / Nigeria / South Africa?"
  • "What is an Employer of Record and do I need one for Africa?"
  • "How does outsourcing to Africa compare to India or Eastern Europe?"
  • "As an Australian company, how can I hire engineers from Africa?"
Content structure for AIO
  • First sentence = direct answer — no preamble. Just answer. E.g. "Global Careers Africa is a talent platform connecting international employers with pre-vetted African professionals."
  • Use questions as H2 headings — "How does the verification process work?" not "Our Process".
  • Add FAQ schema to every information page — the single highest-impact technical change for AIO.
  • Concrete facts over claims — "GCA vets the top 5% of applicants across a 7-stage process" beats "We find the best talent".

3
GEO Content Programme
Months 4–8 · The core content investment
Priority: High

This is the highest-leverage phase. The research is clear: standard SEO explains only 4–7% of AI citation variance. What explains the rest is content that is deep, structured, authoritative, and consistently associated with a specific topic.

Constraint reminder: The bottleneck at this stage is content, not tooling. Every week spent evaluating GEO platforms without publishing new content is a week of competitive ground conceded.
The 5 content types AI engines cite most
Type 1

Definition pages

Clearly explain core concepts. First sentence = the definition. These become the pages AI cites when a user asks "what is X?"

GCA examples: "What is an EOR in Africa?" · "What is the Global Careers Africa Programme?" · "What is smartsourcing?" · "What does talent verification mean?"

Type 2

Comparison pages

AI systems frequently generate "top platforms include X, Y, Z" answers. Comparison content positions GCA within these lists.

GCA examples: "Hiring African talent vs. India vs. Eastern Europe" · "GCA vs. Deel vs. Remote for African hiring" · "Best ways to hire remote talent in Africa (2026)"

Type 3

Process / how-to guides

Decision-stage content that maps directly to the prompts buyers type into AI. Long-form, step-by-step, with numbered sections that AI can extract cleanly.

GCA examples: "How to hire remote developers in Kenya from Australia" · "How to build a global team from Africa compliantly" · "How the GCA verification process works — 7 stages explained"

Type 4

Data and benchmark pages

AI systems strongly favour original data, statistics, and structured facts. Even rough-but-honest benchmarks are more citable than marketing claims.

GCA examples: "African professional salary benchmarks 2026" · "Talent availability by country: Kenya, Nigeria, South Africa, Ghana" · "Remote hiring timelines: Africa vs. traditional markets"

Type 5

Glossary / knowledge base

Individual term pages with DefinedTerm schema are a GEO cheat code. Each page owns one query. High citation probability with relatively low content effort.

GCA examples: "Employer of Record (EOR) Africa" · "Remote hiring compliance Africa" · "Work permits: Kenya vs. Nigeria vs. South Africa" · "Cross-border payroll Africa"

Authority and Off-Site Signals
Months 7–11 · Amplifies Phase 3 content reach
Priority: Medium-High

AI systems evaluate more than your website. They assess how consistently your brand is associated with specific topics across the wider web — partner sites, press coverage, forums, LinkedIn, and third-party platforms.

Why this matters for GEO: AI citation patterns are influenced by semantic relationships and consistency across sources. When multiple external sources associate GCA with African talent sourcing, AI models strengthen that association.
Off-site authority actions
  • Partner page text alignment — ensure RCSA, Worknation, CCI Global, GIZ partner pages describe GCA in consistent language matching your own positioning.
  • LinkedIn long-form content — publish 2 articles/month mirroring site content topics. LinkedIn is indexed by AI systems (especially Perplexity) and creates a second citation surface.
  • Guest posts on recruitment and HR publications — one bylined article per month in industry press (RCSA magazine, HR Daily, African Business). Each creates a citation surface with a backlink.
  • Podcast and media appearances — Perplexity frequently cites podcast transcripts and news articles. One media appearance per quarter generates citation-worthy content outside your domain.
  • Client case studies — specific, outcome-driven: "How [Company] built a 10-person Africa team in 60 days". Highly citable by AI and valuable for traditional SEO.
  • Wikipedia/Wikidata entity presence — AI models heavily weight Wikipedia-adjacent sources. A well-sourced mention on relevant Wikipedia pages builds entity recognition.

5
Engineering and Automation Layer
Months 10–18 · Only after content foundation is established
Priority: Future

This is the phase where technical capability creates a sustainable competitive edge. It is only appropriate once GCA has 20+ content pages, established AI citation presence, and a clear understanding of which prompts drive citations.

Sequencing matters: Tools like LangChain, LlamaIndex, and prompt-tracking scripts are powerful — but they optimise content that already exists. The engineering phase amplifies Phase 3 content; it cannot replace it.
Automated prompt tracking

Replace manual weekly testing with a script that runs 50+ prompts against ChatGPT and Perplexity APIs and logs citation presence automatically.

  • LangChain — orchestrate multi-prompt testing across ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity APIs. Log: is GCA mentioned? Position in answer? Framing?
  • Python + Google Sheets — store weekly prompt results in a simple spreadsheet. Track citation rate over time.
  • Competitor tracking — same script logs which domains are cited instead of GCA. Identifies content gaps to close.
Semantic content analysis
  • sentence-transformers (Python) — analyse whether GCA content semantically matches the prompts being tracked. Identify topics covered superficially vs. deeply.
  • LlamaIndex — build an internal retrieval layer over GCA content to test AI-readiness before publishing.
AI-readable content endpoints (advanced)
  • /knowledge/ endpoint — a structured page summarising GCA's key facts, programme details, and definitions in clean, parseable format. Feeding AI systems directly.
  • llms.txt file — an emerging standard (similar to robots.txt) that gives AI crawlers structured guidance about your content. Early adoption = early advantage.

Measurement framework

Two Dashboards, One Strategy

Measuring GEO effectiveness requires a fundamentally different approach to SEO. Both dashboards are required — one without the other gives an incomplete picture of how GCA is performing in the evolving search landscape.

Dashboard 1

SEO Metrics

Traditional, platform-supported, reliable attribution.

  • Organic impressions and clicks (Search Console)
  • Keyword rankings for target queries
  • Organic traffic to key pages (GA4)
  • Backlinks and referring domains (Ahrefs)
  • Core Web Vitals scores
  • Schema validation (Rich Results Test)
Dashboard 2

AI / GEO Metrics

Emerging, influence-based, requires custom tracking.

  • Prompt citation rate (% of tracked prompts where GCA is cited)
  • Citation quality (recommended vs. mentioned vs. absent)
  • Brand framing ("trusted partner" vs. "unknown platform")
  • Competitor citation share (who gets cited instead?)
  • AIO appearances for target queries
  • Platform coverage: ChatGPT / Perplexity / Gemini separately
